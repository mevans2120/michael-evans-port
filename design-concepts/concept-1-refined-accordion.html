Overview

In 2013, Virgin America embarked on a mission to create the first truly responsive airline website. At the time, mobile booking was clunky and desktop-first designs dominated the industry.

As the lead UX designer and front-end architect, I spearheaded the redesign that would set a new standard for airline digital experiences.

The Challenge

Airlines were notoriously slow to adopt modern web standards. Most sites required desktop computers for booking, frustrated mobile users, and had conversion rates below 2%.

Virgin America wanted to break this pattern and create a mobile-first booking experience that maintained brand personality while dramatically improving usability.

Results

The responsive redesign won multiple industry awards and became a case study in airline digital innovation. Mobile bookings increased 3x in the first year.

Design Decision Split screen creates clear information hierarchy and guides reading flow. Navigation provides constant context about case study structure and current position.

Concept 1 - Analysis

✓ Strengths

  • Familiar interaction patterns - low learning curve for users
  • Smooth, polished animations create premium feel
  • Split screen navigation provides excellent content wayfinding
  • Accordion keeps users engaged on homepage, reduces navigation friction
  • Professional aesthetic that works for corporate/enterprise contexts

⚠ Tradeoffs

  • Traditional patterns - doesn't push creative boundaries
  • Accordion can become cluttered with many expanded projects
  • Split screen navigation takes up valuable horizontal space
  • May feel conservative compared to more experimental designs
