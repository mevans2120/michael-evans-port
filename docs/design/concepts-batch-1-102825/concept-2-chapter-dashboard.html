CHAPTER_01: Understanding the Problem

The Challenge

Most of Virgin America's revenue came from business travelers—frequent flyers who pay premium prices and book last-minute. They'll figure out how to use any booking system.

But leisure travelers, who book 2-3 times per year, were struggling. An airline ticket is one of the most complex consumer products to purchase.

Leisure travelers were failing to convert at alarming rates.

→ The brief: make it simpler for leisure travelers without compromising the experience for business travelers.

CHAPTER_02: Key Insight

Clicks vs. Decisions

In 2014-2015, the industry was obsessed with reducing clicks to improve conversion. We discovered something more fundamental:

It's not about the number of clicks—
it's about the number of decisions.

Combining multiple decisions into one screen to reduce clicks actually makes the experience more complicated and overwhelming. Each decision needs space to breathe.

CHAPTER_03: Implementation

A Step-by-Step Booking Flow

We redesigned the entire booking flow around individual decisions, with each step optimized independently.

  • Who's going — Clear passenger selection
  • Dates and times — Focused date picker without distractions
  • Flights — Present options with clear differentiation
  • Seats — Visual seat selection
  • Traveler information — Simplified form entry
  • Confirmation — Clear summary and payment

This pattern became widely adopted in e-commerce and is now considered standard practice for complex purchase flows.

CHAPTER_06: Design Concept Analysis

Concept 2: Chapter Dashboard

Strategy: Transform case studies into a dashboard-like experience with persistent side navigation and chapter-based structure.

Strengths: Easy to jump between sections, feels more technical and data-driven, side navigation provides constant context, works well for users who want to explore non-linearly.

Tradeoffs: Requires more screen real estate (less mobile-friendly), may feel too technical for non-technical case studies, side nav takes up valuable horizontal space.

// Visual Language Choices:
- Grid pattern background: Evokes data visualization and technical precision
- Neon cyan accents: Fresh alternative to purple, references terminal UIs
- Monospace typography: Creates authority, references code editors
- Dashboard-style stats: Makes metrics feel more analytical
- Terminal-style headers: Reinforces technical, developer-focused aesthetic
